Parts of Speech

Noun

The name of a person, place, object or idea; the name is what something is referred as.

Example: My name is Roger.
Roger is the name of the person.

Verb

The action, state, or occurrence of a person, place object or idea; what happened, is or something someone did.

Example: Roger flexed his biceps
Flexing is the action Roger did.

Adjective

A word that describes a person, place object or idea; a word that specifies what something is.

Example: Roger is a fast runner.
Fast describes Roger as a runner.

Adverb

A word that describes the action, state or occurrence of a person, place or object; specifies the action of something

Example: Roger slowly lifts the box.
Slowly describes the person's action.

Punctuation

Full stop

A dot near the bottom of a word put at the end of a sentence to separate different topics.

Example: I want to go out with my friends today. Also, can you put the ice cream in the fridge?

The full stop (.) separates the topic of the outside and ice cream.

Comma

A symbol that works like a full stop, mainly used to list things and extend sentences.

Examples: While you put the ice cream in, my water bottle, sandwich, and chocolate bar is also in the fridge.

The commas (,) list out different items in the fridge.

Is it okay if you could give me those, as well as getting my phone on the table?

The comma extended the sentence.

Apostrophe

A single speech mark used to show who owns something or shortens a phrase.

Examples: No, that’s Roger’s phone. My phone’s the one on the right.

The first apostrophe (‘) was used to show Roger owned a phone.
The second and third apostrophe combined “is” into a single word.

Speech marks

Double speech marks (“) used to show dialogue between people.

Examples: Person 1: “Thanks for giving my stuff to me, you’re so nice!”

Person 2: “You’re welcome!”

Parentheses

Also called brackets, used to add context to a sentence

Example: Here, you will see the beautiful Gabilan range (Above the mountains)
